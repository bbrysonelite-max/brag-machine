## Secondary CTA (sparingly)

**Energy:** Open a conversation, not a sale.  
**Template:** "If you run a team and want to see what this looks like for your group — let's talk."

Use this sparingly, only when the post naturally lands on leader/white-label territory. This is the door for:
- Leaders wanting to see Tiger for their whole downline
- Prospective white-label partners
- People already in the network-marketing space who sense a team-scale opportunity

Do NOT use this as the primary CTA. Reserve it for relationship-stage posts and DM follow-up, never cold outreach.

## Warm-up mode (account acceleration)

During account warm-up, prefer **reach-safe CTA mechanics** over raw outbound links (algorithms throttle on cold accounts):

- **Comment-to-get:** "Drop a comment below if you want the checklist" or "Reply and I'll send it to you."
- **DM keyword:** "DM me 'BLUEPRINT' and I'll send you the link."
- **Soft pointer:** Mention the magnet by name without the link; link lives in the bio.

This buys algorithmic safety while the account is building trust. Once the account is warm (consistent engagement, no throttle), transition to direct links.

## Hard rules

1. **Never post a cold checkout link to Tiger on social.**  
   No `tigerclaw.io/buy`, no product pricing link, no direct "start your trial" URL on cold posts. Tiger is a *warm* conversation, not a cold click.

2. **"Get a Tiger" and "find an opportunity" come AFTER the relationship.**  
   These asks belong in:
   - The nurture email sequence (after opt-in)
   - DM conversations (after engagement)
   - Phone calls (after permission)  
   
   NOT in cold social CTAs. Different audiences (team member / leader / prospect) get segmented messaging downstream.

3. **Magnet URL is the only link in cold posts.**  
   If you're linking on cold social, it's always `stan.store/brentbryson` (the live free-guide opt-in) or the Bio link shortcut ("DM me for the link"). Nothing else. (Never `…/p/factory-blueprint` — that page is not live.)

4. **Every post should serve the email list.**  
   The implicit funnel: post → magnet → email → nurture → conversation → product/partnership. Don't skip the email step.

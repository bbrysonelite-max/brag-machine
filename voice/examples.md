# Gold-Standard Examples

**Date:** 2026-06-17
**Source:** idea bank (`~/Desktop/Social Media Posts: Ideas/ideas-*.md`)

> **Caution:** These set the BAR, not a template. Vary the opening move — do NOT reuse the "Most [X] don't have a [Y] problem" or "You're not a [role], you're a [role]" hook shapes.

These posts anchor the quality bar for the write-content skill. Each one passes all five rubric criteria — Voice, Compliance, Frame, Platform, CTA. When in doubt, match the cadence, the team-first frame, and the soft CTA you see here. Do not ship anything weaker than these.

Rubric (every post must pass all five):
1. **Voice** — ≥1 named cadence, short declaratives, no banned words, no exclamation padding.
2. **Compliance** — passes `never-say.md` (no income/health/guarantee/endorsement claims; Brent = biography only).
3. **Frame** — speaks to "your team"; team-first, not me-first; ties to follow-up/coaching.
4. **Platform** — matches that platform's format rules.
5. **CTA** — lead-magnet primary or warm-up reach-safe; never a cold Tiger checkout link.

## Example 1 — Facebook

**Pillar:** Make your team more effective
**Source idea:** "The machine your team is missing is follow-up" (Customer Acquisition Machine.jpg)
**Cadence used:** Reframe Correction ("That's not [X]. That's [Y].")

> Most teams think they have a lead problem.
>
> They don't. They have a follow-up problem. Leads come in, a builder gets busy, and the conversation quietly dies in the inbox. That's not a lead problem. That's a follow-up problem.
>
> The fortune is in the follow-up. A system that never drops a conversation keeps every prospect warm — so the work your team puts in up front doesn't leak out the back.
>
> Want the map for how that follow-up line actually runs? Comment "BLUEPRINT" below and I'll send you the Factory Blueprint.

**Why this passes:**
- **Voice:** Reframe Correction lands the turn ("That's not a lead problem. That's a follow-up problem."). Short declaratives. No banned words, no exclamation padding.
- **Compliance:** Value is framed as "conversations kept alive," never dollars or earnings. No guarantee, no company name, no health claim.
- **Frame:** Speaks to "your team" and "a builder," ties directly to follow-up — Tiger's heart. No me-first hustle energy.
- **Platform:** FB format — one punchy hook line, two short paragraphs of context, soft CTA, zero hashtags (under the 0–2 cap).
- **CTA:** Comment-to-get ("BLUEPRINT") — warm-up reach-safe, points at the lead magnet, no cold checkout link.

## Example 3 — X (thread)

> 1/ You can't be on five calls at once. Your training can.
>
> 2/ Record the lesson once. Your team hears it the same way on day one and day ninety. That's not a recording. That's duplication.
>
> 3/ You're not here to repeat yourself fifty times. You're here to lead the people who are moving.
>
> 4/ Your hours go to coaching. The system handles the in-between.
>
> 5/ That's the Factory Blueprint — train once, duplicate everywhere. (link in bio)
>
> #AIForTeams #Leadership

**Why this passes:**
- **Voice:** Parallel Inversion in 3/ ("You're not here to repeat yourself... You're here to lead..."); Reframe Correction in 2/ ("That's not a recording. That's duplication."). Declarative, signal over story — X's expectation. No banned words, no exclamation padding.
- **Compliance:** Frames the value as duplicable training and reclaimed hours — no earnings, no guarantee, no company name, no health claim.
- **Frame:** Every post speaks to "your team" / "your training"; ties to coaching and duplication. Repels the me-first reader by putting the leader's hours on the team.
- **Platform:** X thread of 5 short posts, each well under 280 characters; 2 hashtags (at the cap); soft link only at the natural end.
- **CTA:** Soft magnet pointer ("link in bio") at the close — lead-magnet-first, reach-safe, no cold checkout link.

> So you record it once and let it do the repeating. It never gets tired. It never skips a step. It never sounds annoyed on the hundredth run. Your hours go back to the people who are actually moving — the ones worth coaching in real time.
>
> That's not replacing you. That's protecting the part of you only you can give.
>
> Want the order to teach it in? Get the Factory Blueprint — link in the description.
>
> #AI #Leadership #Teams

**Why this passes:**
- **Voice:** Anaphoric Stack ("It never gets tired. It never skips a step. It never sounds annoyed...") drives the drumbeat; closes with a Reframe Correction. Educational, authoritative tone fits YouTube. No banned words, no exclamation padding.
- **Compliance:** Value is duplicable training and reclaimed coaching hours — no earnings, guarantee, company name, or health claim.
- **Frame:** Speaks to "your team" and "the new builder"; ties to coaching and training duplication, not me-first hustle.
- **Platform:** YouTube format — hook (what the idea solves) → value → soft magnet pointer in the description body; ~150 words; no hashtag-discovery spam (3 description tags, not hashtag stuffing).

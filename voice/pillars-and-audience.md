## The spine

**Question that drives every post:**  
"What will Tiger do for your team?"

Look past the individual — speak to leverage at the **team** level (current team: everybody in it; or future team). Assume the reader is smart enough to want a Tiger. Don't sell down; speak up. The desire being served: **AI that is safe, duplicable, and real leverage / real value.** Where Tiger delivers that: the **follow-up and coaching** part of the program ("Fortune is in Follow-up" + duplication doctrine — "give them their own Tiger").

This is leader-first positioning: the leverage is leaders (a leader = their whole team + white-label potential), not individual tool sales. Tiger is the **proof**, not the pitch.

---

## Audience filter

**Target:** Leaders who think about their team, not themselves.  
**Canonical phrase:** "My people: people who don't think about themselves, they think about their team."

**What this means for content:**
- **Repel** the me-first individual (the person optimizing for their own hustle, ego, or quick win).
- **Attract** the team-first leader (the person optimizing for their whole team's duplication, safety, and growth).

**Drumbeat:** Always say "your team." If you're tempted to say "you," rephrase it around the team. Content should make team-first leaders nod and make me-first individuals uncomfortable.

## The four pillars

### 1. **Inform your team**
*Keep a team current on AI without overwhelm.*

**Example angle:** "Your team doesn't need to learn everything about AI. They need to learn the right five things." (From idea bank: "The AI skills your team will need next year" — a roadmap that names prompting, context, knowledge, reasoning, tools. A leader's job isn't to make everyone an engineer. It's to keep the team current without drowning them.)

---

### 2. **Train your team**
*AI as a duplicable training / coaching layer.*

**Example angle:** "You can't be on five calls at once. Your training can." (From idea bank: "Clone the leader, not the work" — the leader records the lesson once; the team gets it on demand. Duplication you can't do with your calendar.)

---

### 3. **Make your team more effective**
*Follow-up + leverage (Tiger's heart).*

**Example angle:** "Most teams don't have a lead problem. They have a follow-up problem." (From idea bank: "The machine your team is missing is follow-up" — a system that never drops a conversation. The fortune is in the follow-up, and that's exactly where AI earns its keep.)

---

### 4. **Save your team money / time**
*Real value, "way more value than it costs."*

**Example angle:** "Stop paying for the same lesson twice." (From idea bank: "One shelf, the whole team" — when every leader keeps their own scattered notes, the team relearns the same thing over and over. A single organized library saves the hours each new person spends figuring out what already exists.)

---

## Voice rules for these pillars

Tone: biography-as-credibility (never earnings), focus outside Brent, "value must exceed cost."

## Creative Laws (from /brag, adopted)

1. **15–25 seconds.** Not one second more without a reason.
2. **Hook in the first 2–3 seconds** — pattern interrupt, Brent-style. Plan it first.
3. **Pattern:** Hook → Reveal → 2–3 sharp highlights → Punchline/outro.
4. **Show the thing** — at least one scene with real product UI or a recreated one. No abstract filler.
5. **No generic SaaS language.** "Streamline your workflow" is banned. Use real Tiger Claw copy.
6. **Readable:** pace through motion and cuts, never flashing text. Short label ≥0.8s settled; sentences ~0.3s/word. Fast-in, then HOLD.
7. **Beat-sync:** lock 1–3 major reveals to strong music cues (±0.15s); snap sequential items to the beat grid (±0.10s); every-other-beat for text chips at fast tempos.
8. **Funny earns its place** — humor from truth, not from trying.

## TYPE-SIZE LAW (Brent, 2026-07-23 — verified on social: small text is unreadable on phones, big text is great)

Minimums on the 1080-wide vertical canvas (scale proportionally for other formats):
- Hook/display lines: **≥100px**, weight 800-900
- The key statement (quote, law, message line): **≥64px** — this is the line the viewer came for; when in doubt, BIGGER
- Support/secondary lines and chips: **≥54px**
- Narration/context lines: **≥70px**
- Tiny "authenticity detail" text (file receipts, date-chip captions) may stay small — it is texture, never load-bearing reading
Nothing the viewer MUST read may render below 54px. Bigger beats prettier.

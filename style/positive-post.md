# Positive Post Style

Light, kind, human. Gratitude, a win worth sharing, a shout-out, encouragement, a personal note. The feel-good lane of the machine — warmth without syrup.

## Brand

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Background | `#0E1218` softened dark, warm glow (`rgba(255,190,130,0.12)`) drifting slowly |
| Text | `#F5F7FA` primary · `#D8E0E8` secondary |
| Accent | soft gold `#FFD9A0 → #FFB36B` — gentler than the promo ember |
| Font | Inter — 800 for the heart of the message, 400 for the rest. Generous line-height |
| No logo, no product, no urgency | nothing is being sold |

## Creative Laws

1. **12–20 seconds.** Short and warm beats long and impressive.
2. **Hook = the human line** ("Somebody believed in you once." / "Becca. This one's for you.") in the first 2s.
3. **Pattern:** the human line → the message unfolds gently (line-by-line, soft fades and drifts) → HOLD on the heart of it → soft sign-off.
4. **Motion is gentle:** slow fades, soft drifts, easy scale. No snap-zooms, no kinetic slams. It should feel like a breath, not a beat-drop.
5. **Sincerity test:** if the line could be a greeting card cliché, sharpen it until it sounds like a real person said it.
6. **Readable:** long holds, big soft type. Never rush an emotional line.
7. **Beat-sync is loose here** — align to the music's phrasing, not every beat.
8. **Sign-off:** small and personal ("Love, Dad" / "— Brent" / nothing at all). CTA only if explicitly requested.

## Voice

Warm, plain, specific. Specific beats grand: "you always call your grandma back" lands harder than "you have a beautiful soul."
